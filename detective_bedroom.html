The room is large and lofty, befitting someone of royal status. The windows, long and narrow, provide an undisturbed view of the countryside that the Desdemona family owned, stretching on for miles. Comfortless, antique furniture decorates the room. Center of it all, laying on a bed fit for any king, is Mahlon. Ezra almost doesn't recognize him. In his every memory, Mahlon is filled with a vitality that rivalled the sun. A shining light that persisted in a place determined to snuff it out. Laying there with his hands folded over his chest, pale and motionless, he seems almost drained of life.
